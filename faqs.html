What's on your mind?

Free trials. Membership. Class times. Dance styles. Eligibility.
All common things we get asked about, and all answered below.

Money

Can I try before I buy?

Definitely! The first lesson for anyone that's not been before is free, so you can see what we do and consider whether or not you'd like to come again.

Do I need to purchase membership?

If you've tried it and want to carry on, you will need one as the price includes insurance - you are highly unlikely to get injured, but it does very occasionally happen. Members also receive discounts that make classes work out cheaper!

Do I need a gym (SSP) membership too?

Nope - as we are not a Surrey Sports Park club, you don't need a gym membership to join us. Our one-off membership fee is a fraction of the cost, and goes straight back into providing more for our members.

Classes

What is the beginners' class?

The beginner classes are aimed at absolute beginners and will gradually work through various dances with progression through the year.

And what's accelerated beginners?

The accelerated beginners class runs at a faster rate for dancers with some previous experience, or for anyone who's particularly eager to learn.

I've got a fair amount of prior experience

Then ask us about the advanced class, which can cater for everyone from IVDA "Novice" level (who are moving up from Beginners) to the very advanced.

Can I get private lessons?

Definitely, but for those you'll need to get in contact with our teachers directly. Go to their website or ask them in person for more details.

Is there any practice outside lessons?

There is indeed, and it normally happens on Thursday evenings. It's a completely free session with no teachers for you to go over anything you're not sure about. The exact time and location is announced each week on our mailing list and on Facebook.

Anything else?

Yes - our teachers have their own dance school, and teach in Basingstoke on Tuesday evenings and Camberley on Thursday evenings. If you can get to either of them, it's a useful extra.

Eligibility

I'm not a (Surrey) student, can I still dance?

As a student club part funded by the Student's Union our priority is to offer dancing to students, but do ask - membership for non-students is at the discretion of the committee.

What standard do I have to be?

Any! We cater for complete beginners with no coordination, to the very advanced. Our teachers can teach you from any level, to any level.

What if I'm struggling?

Everyone struggles at the beginning, and even after the going gets tough sometimes. The best advice to you if you really want to dance is to STICK AT IT - it takes time, but in the long run you'll have the confidence to dance to almost anything!

Other questions

What dance styles are there?

We offer Ballroom, Latin American, and (depending on participation) Rock n' Roll and Offbeat as extras.

What are the medals?

At the end of the year you are able to take an ISTD medallist exam which will give you a more permanent memento of your achievements with us that you can put on your CV. Contact us for more information.

I've got a question not answered here

Then drop us an email or find us on Facebook, and we'll see if we can answer it. If we don't know the answer ourselves, we'll pass it along to someone who does.
