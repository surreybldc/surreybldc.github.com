Who we are

We are the University of Surrey's Ballroom and Latin American dance club. Since 1998 we've offered competitions, classes, socials, performances and a whole lot more inbetween to all of our members.

All students welcome

That's right - any current student at Surrey can join, and no gym membership is required so it's cheap too. Your first session is always free, so give it a go! And whether you can only make it once a week or you want to go all out, we've got something for you.

Do I need prior experience?

Not at all! Whether you've been a dancer for years or you don't know your left foot from your right foot, you'll always be welcome and we have something to suit every level of ability.

We offer modern ballroom...

Viennese Waltz

An elegant and fast-flowing dance born in Vienna, which the aristocracy danced to music by Strauss and other classical composers. The steps are simple, but at double the speed of the "English" slow waltz it's all about the stamina!

Tango

A passionate Argentinian dance originally banned for being too scandalous, characterized by a cat-like walking action - our teacher Vicki describes it as "a wholly different animal" to the other dances.

Waltz

A slow, romantic ballroom style dance which started in Vienna, and later developed into the Slow English Waltz. Danced to a signature 3/4 beat.

Quickstep

A fun and lively English style ballroom dance with fast movement, including a variety of hops, kicks, skips, lock steps and chasses.

Foxtrot

Characterised by silky smooth, flowing, walking-style movements, and danced to the likes of Frank Sinatra.

...and Latin American...

Cha cha

An upbeat, feisty, and syncopated dance, deriving its name and character from its distinct repetitive foot rhythm. Usually danced to pop and club music.

Jive

A popular, fast and exhilarating dance originating in the 50's from the American GI's, and often danced to rock 'n' roll music.

Rumba

The fascinating rhythms and sexually provocative hip and body expressions make this one of the most sensuous Latin dances.

Paso Doble

A dramatic "Spanish bullfight", where the man plays the bull and the lady his cape. Usually danced to "Espana Cani", the Spanish Gypsy Dance.

Samba

A rhythmical and distinctive Brazilian dance combining traditional Latin hip action with a trademark "bounce".

...and a few other styles too!

Availability

The following styles are offered depending on the demand from our members, but we always do our best to deliver them.

Rock 'n' roll

Non-acrobatic rock 'n' roll is a more energetic and freestyle variant of the jive (above), including kicks, spins, twists and cross-steps. We did say "energetic" - once you've danced rock n' roll, you'll know you've had a serious workout.

Offbeat

If you've done any jazz dance before, you'll feel right at home! This is a more freeform and general dance style that contrasts strongly with the more prescribed "choreography syllabus" of ballroom or Latin.

Compete with us and fly the flag for Team Surrey

Each year we take part in various inter-varsity competitions, where—if you'd like to—you get the opportunity to show off all of your dance skills that you've learned! Anyone can take part, and competitors in each category are roughly matched by ability to keep things fair.

Need to know even more? Read the extended guide to competing with us.

Team Surrey at IVDC

Inter-varsity friendlies

Not quite as large as nationals, these are hosted by individual universities and are usually attended by just a few teams from universities who are relatively local to that competition.

At minimum we go to Bath's winter competition, which also happens to be accompanied by a Christmas Ball!

Inter-varsity nationals

They are, principally:

  • the Southern Universities Dance Competition, which serves as a warm-up to...
  • the Inter Varsity Dance Competition at the Winter Gardens Ballroom, Blackpool, attended by universities up and down the country.

ISTD medallist circuit

You can also join our teachers and their school (Dancerite) at ISTD medallist competitions. These consist of:

  • solo events where you dance with a teacher and are marked on your individual merits;
  • couples events where two students dance together and are marked jointly.
