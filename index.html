Who we are

We are Surrey University's Ballroom and Latin American dance club. Since 1998 we've offered competitions, classes, socials, performances and a whole lot more inbetween to all of our members.

All students welcome

That's right - any current student at Surrey university can join, and no gym membership is required so it's cheap too. Your first session is always free, so give it a go!

Do I need prior experience?

Not at all! Whether you've been a dancer for years or you don't know your left foot from your right foot, you'll always be welcome and we have something to suit every level of ability.

We offer modern ballroom...

Waltz

A slow, romantic ballroom style dance which started in Vienna, and later developed into the Slow English Waltz. Danced to a signature 3/4 beat.

Quickstep

A fun and lively English style ballroom dance with fast movement, including a variety of hops, kicks, skips, lock steps and chasses.

Foxtrot

Characterised by silky smooth, flowing, walking-style movements, and danced to the likes of Frank Sinatra.

Viennese Waltz

An elegant and fast-flowing dance born in Vienna, which the aristocracy danced to music by Strauss and other classical composers.

Tango

A passionate Argentinian dance originally banned for being too scandalous, characterized by a cat-like walking action.

...and Latin American...

Cha cha

An upbeat, feisty, and syncopated dance, deriving its name and character from its distinct repetitive foot rhythm. Usually danced to pop and club music.

Jive

A popular, fast and exhilarating dance originating in the 50's from the American GI's, and often danced to rock 'n' roll music.

Samba

A rhythmical and distinctive Brazilian dance combining traditional Latin hip action with a trademark "bounce".

Rumba

The fascinating rhythms and sexually provocative hip and body expressions make this one of the most sensuous Latin dances.

Paso Doble

A dramatic "Spanish bullfight", where the man plays the bull and the lady his cape. Usually danced to "Espana Cani", the Spanish Gypsy Dance.

...and a few other styles too!

Want to fly the flag for Team Surrey? Join us for a competition...

Each year we take part in various competitions on the university circuit. Some of these are hosted by individual universities; others (like IVDC below) are much larger affairs.

At these competitions you get the opportunity to show off all of your dance skills and dress up to look the part! Anyone can take part, and you always dance against other people at the same level as you. Other than competing in pairs you can also be a part of Surrey's team!

Need to know even more? Read the extended guide to competing with us.

Inter-varsity friendlies

Not quite as large as nationals, these are hosted by individual universities and are usually attended by just a few teams from universities who are relatively local to that competition.

At minimum we go to Bath's winter competition, which also happens to be accompanied by a Christmas Ball in the evening.

Inter-varsity nationals

  • the Southern Universities Dance Competition where all the southern universities (that's every one below Birmingham) join the fray
  • the Inter Varsity Dance Competition (IVDC) at the Winter Gardens Ballroom, Blackpool, which all the universities throughout the country come to

ISTD medallist circuit

Outside of university competitions, you can join our teachers and their school (Dancerite) at ISTD competitions up and down the country. These consist of:

  • solo events where you dance with a teacher and are marked on your individual merits;
  • couples events where two people dance together and are marked jointly.
